Pregnancy-Safe Recipes

Simple, nutritious, vegetarian recipes designed for each meal of the day

Palak Paneer (Spinach & Cottage Cheese Curry)
Serves 2 · 30 minutes · Lunch / Dinner
Vegetarian High Protein Iron Rich Calcium Folate
Ingredients
  • 250g fresh spinach leaves
  • 200g paneer, cubed
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala (mild)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp fresh cream (optional)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
Method
1
Blanch spinach in boiling water for 2 mins, drain and blend into a smooth puree. Set aside.
2
Heat ghee in a pan, add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add onion and sauté until golden brown.
3
Add ginger-garlic paste, cook 1 min. Add tomatoes and cook until soft and oil separates.
4
Add turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Cook the masala for 2 more minutes.
5
Add the spinach puree, mix well, and simmer 5 mins. Gently fold in paneer cubes.
6
Finish with garam masala and cream if using. Serve hot with whole wheat roti.
Moong Dal Khichdi with Vegetables
Serves 2 · 25 minutes · Dinner / Light Meal
Vegetarian Protein Easy Digestion
Ingredients
  • ½ cup moong dal (yellow split)
  • ½ cup rice
  • 1 medium carrot, diced
  • ½ cup green peas
  • ½ cup spinach, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ¼ tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2.5 cups water
  • Fresh coriander to garnish
Method
1
Wash rice and moong dal together. Soak for 15 minutes.
2
Heat ghee in a pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds and hing; let them sizzle.
3
Add diced carrot and peas, stir for 2 minutes. Add turmeric and salt.
4
Add drained rice, dal, and spinach. Mix everything well.
5
Add 2.5 cups water. Pressure cook for 3 whistles on medium heat.
6
Allow pressure to release, open and stir gently. Top with a tsp of ghee and coriander leaves. Serve warm.
Vegetable Oats Upma
Serves 2 · 20 minutes · Breakfast
Vegetarian Iron Folate Fibre
Ingredients
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup spinach, chopped
  • 1 small carrot, grated
  • ¼ cup green peas
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 8–10 curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp ghee or oil
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon juice — ½ lemon
  • Fresh coriander
Method
1
Dry roast oats in a pan for 3–4 minutes until lightly golden and nutty. Set aside.
2
Heat ghee, add mustard seeds and curry leaves. Once they crackle, add onion and sauté until translucent.
3
Add carrots and peas; cook for 3 minutes. Add turmeric and salt.
4
Add 1.5 cups water and bring to a boil. Add roasted oats and spinach, stirring continuously.
5
Cook on low heat for 4–5 minutes until oats absorb water and are soft.
6
Squeeze lemon juice, garnish with coriander. Serve hot with a glass of milk.
Ragi (Finger Millet) Laddoo
Makes 12–14 ladoos · 30 minutes · Snack / Evening
Vegetarian Calcium Rich Iron Protein
Ingredients
  • 1 cup ragi flour
  • ½ cup roasted peanuts, coarsely crushed
  • 3 tbsp jaggery powder (or to taste)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • ¼ tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds (til), roasted
  • 2 tbsp desiccated coconut (optional)
  • Warm water as needed to bind
Method
1
Dry roast ragi flour on low-medium heat for 8–10 minutes, stirring continuously until fragrant. Do not burn.
2
Transfer to a bowl. Add crushed peanuts, roasted sesame, jaggery, cardamom, and coconut.
3
Add warm ghee and mix well. The mixture should have a crumbly, sandy texture.
4
Add warm water very gradually, 1 tsp at a time, kneading until you can shape the mixture into firm balls.
5
Roll into small lemon-sized laddoos. Press firmly. Store in an airtight box at room temperature.
Sprouted Moong Chaat
Serves 2 · 10 minutes · Snack / Mid-Morning
Vegetarian Protein Folate
Ingredients
  • 1.5 cups sprouted moong
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • ½ cucumber, diced
  • ½ small onion, finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • ¼ tsp roasted cumin powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • 1 green chilli (optional, very mild)
  • ½ tsp chaat masala (optional)
Method
1
Steam sprouted moong for 5 minutes (light steaming; they should remain slightly crunchy, not mushy).
2
Allow to cool slightly. Transfer to a mixing bowl.
3
Add tomato, cucumber, onion, and fresh coriander. Mix gently.
4
Add lemon juice, cumin powder, salt, and black pepper. Toss to combine.
5
Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately or refrigerate for up to 2 hours.
6
Note: Always steam sprouts during pregnancy — avoid eating raw sprouts as they may carry bacteria.
Haldi Doodh (Golden Milk / Turmeric Milk)
Serves 1 · 5 minutes · Before Bedtime
Vegetarian Calcium Anti-inflammatory
Ingredients
  • 1 glass (250ml) full-fat milk
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • A pinch of black pepper
  • 3–4 strands of saffron (optional)
  • ½ tsp ghee
  • 1 tsp honey (or jaggery)
  • Pinch of cardamom powder
Method
1
Heat milk in a saucepan on low-medium heat. Do not boil vigorously.
2
Add turmeric, black pepper, saffron, and cardamom. Stir gently.
3
Simmer for 2–3 minutes. The black pepper activates curcumin in turmeric, multiplying its benefits.
4
Remove from heat. Add ghee and honey/jaggery. Stir well and pour into a glass.
5
Sip slowly before sleeping. This promotes deep sleep, reduces inflammation, and delivers calcium for baby's bones.

Tips, Reminders & Cautions

Important things to keep in mind during the 5th month

✅ Always eat small, frequent meals

The baby is growing fast in Month 5 and compressing your stomach. Eat 6 small meals instead of 3 large ones. This also prevents heartburn and acidity, which are very common now.

✅ Pair Iron-rich foods with Vitamin C

Always eat iron-rich foods (spinach, dal, ragi) with a Vitamin C source (lemon juice, amla, orange) in the same meal. This doubles iron absorption naturally.

✅ Soak nuts and seeds before eating

Soak almonds overnight and eat in the morning. Soak chia and flaxseeds before adding to food. Soaking removes phytic acid and dramatically improves nutrient absorption.

✅ Steam sprouts — never eat them raw

Raw sprouts can harbour harmful bacteria during pregnancy. Always lightly steam sprouted moong, chickpeas, or any sprouts before eating.

⚠️ Foods to Avoid

Raw papaya (unripe), excessive pineapple, raw sprouts, unpasteurised dairy, very spicy or oily food, caffeine in excess (limit to 1 cup tea/day), packaged & processed food, excess salt, raw or undercooked eggs, street food with unhygienic handling, alcohol entirely.

✅ Take prenatal supplements as prescribed

Food alone may not meet all requirements for iron, folic acid, and Vitamin D. Always take supplements prescribed by your gynaecologist alongside this diet plan. Do not self-medicate.

✅ Listen to your body's cravings & aversions

Cravings and aversions during pregnancy are normal. If you feel nauseous with certain foods, replace them with nutrient-equivalent alternatives from this guide. Don't force yourself to eat anything that triggers discomfort.

✅ Gentle movement and sunlight

A 20–30 minute gentle walk in morning sunlight provides Vitamin D naturally. Pair with prenatal yoga if cleared by your doctor. Good sleep (7–8 hours) is as vital as good food.

👩‍⚕️ Always Consult Your Doctor

This guide is for general reference. Every pregnancy is unique. Always follow the specific dietary advice given by your gynaecologist or registered dietitian, especially if you have gestational diabetes, anaemia, hypertension, or any other condition.
